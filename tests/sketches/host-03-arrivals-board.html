Arrivals & Waitlist

Model the way a host actually thinks: who's next. Three live columns — Expected (bookings due), Waitlist (walk-ins), Seated (on the floor now). Every walk-in gets an honest wait quote computed from when a fitting table will genuinely free, so you never promise a table that's booked.

Best for: the host stand / door Adds a real waitlist Resy / SevenRooms-class workflow
The insight. Floor plans and timelines are maps; a busy door needs a queue. When someone walks in, the question isn't "which pixel is that table" — it's "can I seat this party, and if not, how long is the wait, and am I about to burn a table Kowalski booked?" This board answers exactly that.

idea Expected → Waitlist → Seated, always live

Bookings flow into Expected as their time nears (with late-flagging). Walk-ins land in Waitlist with a computed quote. Seating from either column links the party to a table and opens its check; that party then lives in Seated with a turn timer until it's cleared.

flow The two broken moments, fixed

SCENARIO A · booking arrives

Kowalski at 19:00

He's already at the top of Expected, flagged "due now." Tap Seat on T2 → he moves to Seated with a live timer, T2's booking becomes occupancy, and his check opens. If T2's still finishing, tap Reassign and the board offers the next fitting-and-free table.

SCENARIO B · walk-in arrives

Party of 6, no booking

Add to Waitlist → the quote engine sees T7 is Mazur's at 19:30 and refuses to quote it. It offers an honest "~45 min, or T9 booth at 19:15." You never verbally promise the one table that's about to be needed — the board did the protecting.

build Under the hood

Medium lift — the columns are views over existing reservations + occupancy, but the waitlist and the quote engine are genuinely new.

New: WaitlistA small entity Waitlist {id, party, prefs, addedAt, quoteMin, status}. The only net-new table in this concept.
Quote enginequote(party, prefs) → {minutes, tableId?}: scan tables, compute each one's next free moment (turn end of current party, or now) minus any reservation that would collide, return the soonest safe fit. This is the same free-window math every concept needs.
Expected feedReservations for today, windowed to "due soon," with late-detection (now > time + graceMin → no-show risk). Seat action = the shared reservation → seated + open check.
Seated feedActive occupancies with turn timers. Clearing frees the table and feeds the waitlist ("T1 is ready → text Walk-in · 2").

trade-offs Honest read

Strengths

  • Matches host mental model; fastest at a busy door.
  • Only concept with a real waitlist + honest wait quotes.
  • Late / no-show handling is first-class.
  • "Text when ready" turns a queue into revenue, not walk-outs.

    • Limits

  • Abstracts away the room — no spatial sense of the floor.
  • Quote accuracy depends on turn-time estimates.
  • New waitlist entity + notify plumbing (SMS/WhatsApp).
  • Best alongside a floor or timeline, not instead of.
