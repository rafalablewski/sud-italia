Unified Host Floor

The floor plan becomes the single seating surface — every table shows who's on it and what's coming for it. Bookings live on the tables, so seating a guest and protecting a held table are the same gesture.

Best for: servers & hosts working the room Merges Book → Floor Ships on today's data model
The break we're fixing. A booking exists in Book (party → table → time), but seating happens in Floor as a generic "seat a party of X." The two never meet: Book doesn't know the table filled, Floor doesn't know a table is spoken for in 30 minutes. Result — you can't answer "who's at T5?" and you can hand a walk-in the exact table Kowalski booked.

idea One board. Tables carry their own future.

Each table tile is a small live dossier: current party (linked to its booking when there is one), a seating timer, and — critically — a "held" ribbon showing the next reservation and its countdown. Incoming arrivals sit in a right-hand rail; drag one onto its table (or tap Seat) to convert reservation → seated and open its check in one move.

flow The two broken moments, fixed

SCENARIO A · booking arrives

Kowalski walks in at 19:00

His card is already glowing in Coming up and T2 wears a "◷ Kowalski · in 2m" ribbon. Tap Seat on T2 (or drag his card onto any open table to reassign). The booking flips to seated, T2 turns blue with his name + timer, and a POS check opens pre-tagged to the booking. Book and Floor now agree: T2 = Kowalski.

SCENARIO B · walk-in arrives

Party of 6, no booking

Tap + Walk-in → 6. The floor lights the tables that seat 6 and have a clear turn ahead. T7 is a 6-top but shows ⚠ Mazur booked 19:30 — overrun, so it's guarded. You seat them on a genuinely-free 6-top instead — the reserved table is protected without you having to remember the book.

build Under the hood

This is the lightest of the five — it rides the data you already have. No new tables in the DB; the reservation already stores tableId, time, durationMin and a seated status.

DataNo schema change. Reservation ↔ table link already exists (reservation.tableId + status). Occupancy = the reservation in seated state, or an ad-hoc walk-in reservation with source:"walk-in" and no name.
New: free-windowfreeWindowMin(table, now) — minutes until the table's next active reservation. Drives the "held" ribbon, the walk-in guard, and the "open all night" chips. Pure function over the day's reservations.
Seat actionPOST /floor/seat {reservationId, tableId} → set reservation seated, set table seated, open a POS check tagged with the reservation. Walk-in: same endpoint, creates the ad-hoc hold first.
UIFold the Book "Coming up" rail and the reservation overlay onto the existing CoreFloor. Book's timeline stays as the planning view; Floor becomes the live seating view. Both read the same reservations.

trade-offs Honest read

Strengths

  • Fastest to ship — days, not weeks; no migration.
  • Spatial & intuitive: servers already think in "which table."
  • The held-ribbon kills the walk-in-on-a-reserved-table mistake.
  • Seating instantly links booking ↔ table ↔ check.

    • Limits

  • A floor grid gets busy past ~30 tables / multi-room.
  • Weaker for "who's next over the whole night" (that's Concept 2/3).
  • No formal waitlist for over-capacity walk-ins yet.
  • Free-window is heuristic until turn-times are learned.
