Concept 2 of 5 · Timeline-primary

Live Table Timeline

The reservation timeline stops being a planning tool and becomes the live operational spine. Reservations, seated parties and walk-ins all render as blocks on one tables×time grid, with a sweeping NOW line. Collisions become impossible to miss — a walk-in that would overrun the next booking literally turns red.

Best for: managers watching the whole service Book's timeline → live floor Reuses the existing timeline UI
The insight. The information a host needs — "is this table really free, and for how long?" — is a time question, not a space question. A floor plan hides time; a timeline makes it the axis. Seating a party is just filling the gap between now and the next block.

idea One axis: tables down, time across, NOW moving

Solid blue blocks are parties actually seated. Dashed amber blocks are reservations not yet arrived. Green blocks are walk-ins, drawn from NOW forward. Drag a booking's edge to NOW to seat it; drop a walk-in into a gap. If a block would overlap the next one, it renders as a red hatch — the system won't let you double-book a table by accident.

SCENARIO A · booking arrives

Kowalski at 19:00

His dashed block on T2 sits right at NOW. Tap it → Seat (or drag its left edge onto the NOW line). It goes solid blue, a check opens, and its right edge shows the expected turn end. The block is the link between booking and occupancy — no second system.

SCENARIO B · walk-in arrives

Party of 6

You start a walk-in block from NOW. As you drop it on T7, it collides with Mazur 19:30 and hatches red — blocked. Drop it on T11 and it's green: a clean 2½-hour runway. The timeline does the "will this fit?" math you'd otherwise do in your head.

build Under the hood

Medium lift: the timeline component already exists in Book. The work is making it consume live occupancy as first-class blocks and adding the NOW line + overlap math.

DataUnify what a "block" is: a reservation (future/dashed) and an occupancy (seated/solid) share one shape {tableId, start, end, party, guest, kind}. Occupancy end = seatedAt + expectedTurnMin. Small: add seatedAt to a seated reservation; a walk-in is a block with no reservation row (or an ad-hoc one).
Live NOWA ticking clock line; blocks left of it are history, right of it are forecast. Auto-scroll to keep NOW centred during service.
Overlap enginecollides(blockA, blockB) on the same table row → red hatch + block the drop. Same primitive Book already uses for double-booking; extended to walk-in placement.
UIPromote Book's core-book-tlpanel into the live Service surface. Add drag-to-seat, drag-between-rows to reassign, and a floor-plan companion view for spatial lookups.

trade-offs Honest read

Strengths

  • Unbeatable for time-of-turn awareness & collision prevention.
  • One mental model for the whole night — great for managers.
  • Reuses the timeline you've already built.
  • Reassign, seat, and forecast are the same drag gesture.

    • Limits

  • Less intuitive for a server who thinks spatially (needs a floor companion).
  • Turn-time estimates matter — a wrong expectedTurnMin mis-colours collisions.
  • Dense on a phone; really wants a tablet/landscape.
  • Walk-in "waitlist when nothing fits" still needs Concept 3.
    • Effort medium · pairs with a floor companion Next → Concept 3 · Arrivals Board
